-- PN vs BOAL: units with largest deviations in period 35
SELECT
  p.bm_unit_id,
  p.level_to AS pn_mw,
  b.bid_offer_level_to AS boal_mw,
  round(b.bid_offer_level_to - p.level_to, 1) AS deviation_mw
FROM silver.pn p
JOIN silver.boal b USING (settlement_date, settlement_period, bm_unit_id)
WHERE p.settlement_date = '2026-05-01'
  AND p.settlement_period = 35
ORDER BY abs(deviation_mw) DESC LIMIT 20;

Caveats

Things to know.

Dataset-specific notes. Also see Elexon-wide caveats.

01
Both settlementDate AND settlementPeriod required

The PN API endpoint returns 404 if you send only settlementDate. The connector iterates all 48 periods explicitly. Do not attempt to bulk-query by date range — there is no from/to parameter for this endpoint.

02
Highest volume Elexon dataset

At 5.4M rows/month, PN is 4x the size of BOAL. Always filter to specific dates or units before loading. Consider materialising aggregates rather than querying raw PN repeatedly.

03
level_from and level_to may be identical

Most units declare a flat output (level_from == level_to). A difference between them indicates a planned ramp within the settlement period.
