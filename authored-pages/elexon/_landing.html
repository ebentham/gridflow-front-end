About the BMRS

Elexon-wide caveats.

Quirks that cut across the 33 datasets below — settlement-period arithmetic, the seven-run revision cadence, BM unit identification, and the open vocabulary of fuel codes. Per-dataset gotchas live on the individual pages.

01
Settlement periods are 1–50, not 1–48

UK settlement uses half-hour buckets indexed from SP 1 at local midnight. DST days carry 46 (spring forward) or 50 (autumn back) periods. Code that hard-codes range(1, 49) loses data twice a year.

02
Seven settlement runs — II is provisional, DF is final

Most data publishes across an II → SF → R1 → R2 → R3 → RF → DF cadence; early runs are provisional and later ones land weeks to months later. Point-in-time joins need the settlement_run dimension — a naïve SELECT MAX silently mixes vintages.

03
BM unit IDs are their own namespace

A single plant can carry a BMU ID (T_DRAXX-1), one or more NGC asset IDs, and a separate metering-system ID. Joins across BMRS endpoints work; joining to NESO regional data or to ENTSO-E PSR types needs an external crosswalk. bmunits_reference is the authoritative BMU metadata table.

04
Fuel-type codes are open-vocabulary

BMRS publishes whatever uppercase token a unit declares — CCGT, COAL, NUCLEAR, INTFR, INTIRL, plus occasional new entries (battery storage arrived without ceremony). Treat the column as a string, not an enum.
